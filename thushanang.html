Sethakga Thushanang

"Thushanang" — from Sesotho, meaning "help one another." Our community giving-back programme provides free and subsidised hair braiding, deep cleaning, and essential support to underprivileged youth and families across Gauteng.

Our Heart

Where It All Began

Sethakga was founded by a woman who grew up understanding what it means to go without. From the very beginning, giving back was never an afterthought — it was part of the DNA.

Thushanang exists because we know that a clean home is a dignified home. A neat hairstyle is confidence. A child who is cared for shows up differently to school, to life, to their own future.

We partner with schools, community centres, and NGOs to identify families and young people who need a hand — not charity, but a genuine act of community care.

How We Help

What Thushanang Provides

Practical, dignified support — not just once-off gestures, but meaningful recurring care

Free Hair Braiding Events

Quarterly free hair braiding days for underprivileged girls and women. Professional Sethakga stylists donate their time and skills to make every person feel beautiful and valued.

Subsidised Deep Cleaning

Families living in difficult conditions — overcrowded homes, post-flooding, or post-illness environments — receive deeply subsidised or free deep cleaning from the Sethakga team.

Free Academy Bursaries

Qualifying youth from under-resourced backgrounds receive fully sponsored places on Sethakga Academy courses — covering the full cost of training and materials.

Back-to-School Support

Each year, Sethakga supports learners returning to school with hair braiding, uniforms where possible, stationery, and hygiene packs — helping children start the year with confidence.

Hygiene & Care Packages

Essential cleaning supplies and hygiene products distributed to families who cannot afford to maintain clean and safe living environments for their children.

Community Partnerships

We partner with schools, shelters, NGOs, and churches to identify and reach those most in need — ensuring support goes where it's truly needed, with dignity at the centre.
